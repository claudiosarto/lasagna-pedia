Welcome to our savory haven celebrating the culinary marvel of lasagna!

Dive into layers of history, regional variations, recipes, and tips, savoring this beloved Italian dish's timeless deliciousness.

Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish that has a rich history dating back many centuries. The origins of lasagna are somewhat debated, and its history is intertwined with the history of pasta in Italy.

Ancient Roots

The concept of layered pasta dishes has ancient roots. Some believe that the Ancient Greeks had a dish similar to lasagna, while others trace it back to Ancient Rome, where they enjoyed a dish called "Laganum," which consisted of layers of pasta and sauce.

Medieval Italy

The first recorded recipe for a dish resembling modern lasagna can be found in an English cookbook from the 14th century, where it is called "loseyn." However, it's important to note that at this time, pasta was not made with tomatoes, as tomatoes were not introduced to Europe until after the exploration of the Americas.

Introduction of Tomatoes

Tomatoes are a key ingredient in modern lasagna, but they were not part of the original recipe. Tomatoes are native to the Americas and were brought to Europe by explorers in the 16th century. Initially, tomatoes were met with suspicion and were thought to be poisonous. It took some time for tomatoes to be widely accepted and incorporated into Italian cuisine

Evolution in Italy

Over time, lasagna evolved in Italy, with regional variations emerging. Different regions developed their own unique styles, with variances in ingredients and preparation methods

Global Spread

Italian immigrants brought their culinary traditions, including lasagna, to various parts of the world in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. As a result, lasagna gained popularity beyond Italy and became a beloved dish in many countries.

Modern Variations

Today, lasagna is enjoyed in countless variations around the world. While the basic concept of layered pasta and sauce remains, there are endless possibilities for the choice of ingredients, including various types of pasta, meats, cheeses, and sauces.
