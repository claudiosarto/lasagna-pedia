Traditional Lasagna Recipe

Traditional lasagna from Emilia Romagna often features a hearty meat sauce, béchamel (white sauce), pasta, and Parmigiano Reggiano cheese. Here's a basic recipe for traditional Lasagna

Ingredients

For the Bolognese Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 500gr (1 lb) ground beef
  • 250gr (1/2 lb) ground pork
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cans (800g) crushed tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Béchamel Sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • Salt and white pepper to taste
  • Pinch of nutmeg (optional)

Other Ingredients:

  • Lasagna sheets (fresh or dried)
  • Grated Parmigiano Reggiano cheese
  • 300gr (about 3 cups) mozzarella cheese, shredded

Instructions

Bolognese Sauce:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Cook until softened.
  2. Add the ground beef and pork to the pot, breaking it apart with a spoon. Cook until browned.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Pour in the red wine and let it simmer until it reduces by half
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes
  6. Season with salt and pepper
  7. Simmer on low heat for at least 3 hours, stirring occasionally.

Béchamel Sauce:

  1. In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk continuously for a few minutes to make a roux.
  2. Gradually add the milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Cook until the sauce thickens.
  3. Season the béchamel with salt, pepper and nutmeg.
  4. Remove from heat and let it rest

Assembly and cook the Lasagna:

  1. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of Bolognese sauce. Place a layer of lasagna sheets on top.
  2. Add a layer of Béchamel sauce, followed by a layer of grated Parmigiano Reggiano and shredded mozzarella.
  3. Repeat the layers until you run out of ingredients, finishing with a layer of Béchamel and a generous sprinkle of Parmigiano Reggiano and mozzarella.
  4. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  5. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for about 25-30 minutes.
  6. Uncover and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  7. Allow the lasagna to rest for a few minutes before serving.
